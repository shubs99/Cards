Shiva

Shiva is known as "The Destroyer" within the Trimurti, the Hindu trinity which also includes Brahma and Vishnu. Shiva is also known as Adiyogi Shiva, regarded as the patron god of yoga, meditation and the arts.

Durga

Durga is a major Hindu goddess (Devi), worshipped as a principal aspect of the mother goddess Mahadevi. She is associated with protection, strength, motherhood, destruction and war.

Krishna

Krishna is worshipped as the eighth avatar of Vishnu and also as the Supreme god in his own right. He is the god of protection, compassion, tenderness, and love among Indian divinitie.

Narasimha

Narasimha is the fourth avatar of the Hindu god Vishnu. He incarnates in the form of a part-lion, part-man being to slay Hiranyakashipu, and end religious persecution and calamity on earth, thereby restoring dharma.

Ganesha

Ganesha also known as Ganapati, Vinayaka, and Pillaiyar, is one of the best-known and most worshipped deities in the Hindu pantheon. He is widely revered, more specifically, as the remover of obstacles and thought to bring good luck.

Kali

Kali, also referred to as Mahakali, Bhadrakali and Kalika, who is considered to be the goddess of ultimate power, time and change. She is a ferocious form of goddess Mahadevi, the supreme of all powers, or the ultimate reality.

Hanuman

Hanuman is a divine vanara (monkey) companion of the god Rama.He is an ardent devotee of Rama and one of the chiranjivis. Hanuman is also son of the wind-god Vayu.

Lakshmi

Lakshmi, also known as Shri, is one of the principal goddesses in Hinduism. She is the goddess of wealth, fortune, power, beauty, fertility and prosperity. Along with Parvati and Saraswati, she forms the Tridevi of Hindu goddesses.
